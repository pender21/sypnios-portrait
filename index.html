Why We Call It The Portrait
A different kind
of sitting.

In 1954, Sir Winston Churchill, one of the most studied leaders in modern history, agreed to sit for a portrait. Not a photograph. A portrait.

He spent time with the artist. He was observed. He was known. When the portrait was finally revealed, Churchill was not pleased with what he saw. The artist had not painted the image Churchill had spent a lifetime curating. He had painted the man he had actually come to know.

That is the difference between a photograph and a portrait. A photograph captures an image. A portrait reveals a person.

This is a different kind of sitting. We are not here to capture the image you have built. We are here to show you who you have always been, and to walk with you toward the fullest version of that person.

What you see today may surprise you. It is meant to. Not to define you. To free you.

Take a seat. We are preparing the canvas.

A Word Before You Begin
This is not an evaluation.
It is an invitation.

Most assessments ask what you have done. This is not one of those.

The Sypnios Leadership Portrait is a discovery instrument, designed not to evaluate your performance, but to reveal your person. "You are not starting from zero. You are returning to something that was always true about you."
